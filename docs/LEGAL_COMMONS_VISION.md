# Legal Commons - Vision Document

## The Dream

**GitHub Repository:** `legal-commons`

Legal Commons envisions a world where quality legal services are accessible to everyone, not just those who can afford expensive attorney fees. We're building an open-source, community-owned platform that democratizes access to legal document preparation, filing assistance, and legal education.

For too long, three major players—LegalZoom (est. 2001), Rocket Lawyer (est. 2008), and Nolo (est. 1971)—have dominated the online legal services market, generating hundreds of millions in revenue annually while still leaving millions of Americans underserved. Solo practitioners, small business owners, immigrants, low-income families, and rural communities face:

- **Price barriers**: $300+ for simple LLC formation that costs $50 in state fees
- **Complexity barriers**: Legal jargon without adequate explanation or guidance
- **Access barriers**: No multilingual support, limited mobile access, poor rural connectivity options
- **Trust barriers**: Corporate-owned platforms prioritizing upsells over user needs
- **Knowledge barriers**: No connection to community legal aid or pro bono networks

Legal Commons changes this by creating a **community-owned, transparent, and accessible platform** that puts people before profits.

## How We Tweak the Model

### 1. **Transparent, Cost-at-Service Pricing**
- **Incumbents:** Hidden fees, aggressive upselling, subscription lock-in
- **Legal Commons:** Pay only state filing fees + optional donation to platform sustainability

### 2. **Community Governance**
- **Incumbents:** Venture-backed, profit-driven corporations
- **Legal Commons:** AGPL-3.0 licensed, community-driven roadmap, open financial reports

### 3. **Mobile-First + Offline-Capable**
- **Incumbents:** Desktop-optimized, requires constant internet
- **Legal Commons:** Progressive Web App with offline document preparation, rural connectivity in mind

### 4. **Privacy-Respecting**
- **Incumbents:** Data harvesting, lead generation, cross-selling
- **Legal Commons:** End-to-end encryption for sensitive documents, no data sales, GDPR/CCPA compliant by design

### 5. **Integration with Public Services**
- **Incumbents:** Isolated commercial platforms
- **Legal Commons:** APIs for legal aid organizations, court self-help centers, library systems

## The Common Folk Manifesto

**Legal Commons is built by the people, for the people.**

We believe that:

1. **Justice should not be a luxury good.** Legal knowledge and tools should be freely accessible to all.

2. **Transparency builds trust.** Our code, our finances, and our governance are open for inspection.

3. **Community wisdom exceeds corporate knowledge.** Lawyers, paralegals, legal aid workers, and affected communities shape our platform.

4. **Technology should empower, not extract.** We build tools that serve users, not shareholders.

5. **Offline is a right.** Rural, poor, and incarcerated users shouldn't be left behind in the digital divide.

6. **Multilingual is the default.** America speaks hundreds of languages—our platform should too.

7. **Privacy is non-negotiable.** Your legal matters are yours alone. We don't sell data, ever.

8. **Accessibility is not optional.** Disabled users shaped the web—our platform honors that legacy.

9. **Open-source is liberation.** AGPL-3.0 ensures our work remains commons, forever.

10. **Local knowledge matters.** State-specific, city-specific, even neighborhood-specific legal resources make the difference.

## The Path Forward

Legal Commons will launch with a **vertical-by-vertical approach**, starting with the most impactful areas:

**Phase 1 (Months 1-6):** Business Formation & Document Library
**Phase 2 (Months 7-12):** Estate Planning & Family Law
**Phase 3 (Year 2):** Immigration & Housing Law
**Phase 4 (Year 2-3):** Intellectual Property & Employment Law

Each vertical will be **co-designed with affected communities**, beta-tested in real-world legal aid settings, and **launched only when it meets our accessibility and accuracy standards**.
